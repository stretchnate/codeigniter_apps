Our Mission

At smart budget our mission is to help you take control of your finances. We've found that the best way to do this is to help you get organized and give you the ability to manage your money yourself. There are other budgeting websites out there but many of them ask you for your personal or financial information so that they can connect with your bank and download your transactional information and then send you alerts when they think you've overspent on something. That doesn't sound a whole lot like you taking control to us. So we opted for a simpler approach, we don't ask you for personal or financial information, because we don't need it, in fact we don't want it. Instead we give you the tools to manage your finances and then let you \"take control\".

In a nutshell...

Smart Budget converts your bank account into categories. Well, not really, your bank account will still be the same, just more organized.

Creating an Account

After you've registered you'll need to create an account to represent your bank account, piggy bank, mayonaise jar or whatever it is you use to store your cash. Don't worry though, we don't ask for any financial or personal information, we are simply simulating your bank account so we can help you organize it. in fact all we ask for is a name for your account and how often you get paid. The name is so you know which account your working with and the pay schedule is so we can calculate the best way to organize your money.

Creating Categories

Once you have an account created you can begin adding categories. We ask for a little bit more information on these but we are still careful to not ask for any personal or financial data. You can add as many categories as you would like, for example, you might want a category for rent/mortgage, and another for car payment, perhaps groceries and gas would be good to have. you can be as broad or as detailed as you want, whatever helps you take control of your finances.

Adding Funds

Now that you have an account and categories created it's time to add funds. Keep in mind, we're not adding real money we are simply simulating the money that has already been added to your bank account so we can organize it into the categories that have been created.
To add funds simply navigate to Funds -> Add Funds and fill out the form. For your first add it's probably best to simply add the balance of your bank account but that also depends on whether or not you set up your categories with cash already in them.
Once you've given the form a Source, Gross amount and an account to deposit the funds you'll probably notice a little checkbox near the submit button. This checkbox provides you with the ability to categorize your money after it is deposited in your account. If you don't select the checkbox your money will automatically be categorized for you in order of due date (closes first) and priority (highest priority first, where 1 is highest priority).
Our prediction is once you give the automatic categorizing a shot, you'll never turn back.

Manually Adding Funds

If you decide to manually add funds, no big deal, you simply need to navigate to each category that you want to deposit money into, give it an amount and select the \"Add funds from [account name]\", then click the submit button. As long as you have money in your account it will get deposited into your category.

Deductions What do I do when I spend money?

Now for the fun part, when you spend money get a receipt, if it's a check keep the carbon copy (or write down the amount and check number) then on a regular basis log into your account and enter the amounts you spend into their respective categories. Then as often as you see fit log into your bank account and check the balance of your bank account against the \"Account Balance\" on the home page of your smart budget account. If you have entered in your transactions correctly and your bank account is up to date those two numbers should match.
That's it! No more checking your bank account to see if you have money and then doing math in your head to see if you afford that new pair of shoes that you've had your eye on, just check your Smart Budget categories and they will tell you if you can afford whatever it is you are trying to afford.

Refunds Is there an easy way to add a refund?

Yes! Simply go to the category where the original deduction took place and provide a date and amount and then click on the \"Refund\" radio button. Once you do this a new box will pop up asking you for an ID. If you have the original transaction ID great! If not, you can probably find it in the transactions grid near the bottom of the screen. But if you don't want to go through all that work just click submit, we'll still process the refund.

Deleting a transaction Oops, I made a mistake, can I delete that last transaction?

Absolutley! Simply fidn the transaction you wish to delete and click on the big red 'X' at the right. A pop up will ask you to confirm and once you click \"OK\" you'll be good to go.

Transferring Funds Can I move money from one category to the other?

Of course! All you need to do is go to Funds -> Transfer Funds, choose whether you'll be transferring between accounts or categories, select the category to transfer from and the category to transfer to, enter the amount and click submit. As long as you have money in the category you are transferring from it will get transferred, however we won't transfer more than you have in that category.

Notes

What are notes? You can add notes to any or all pages on your Smart Budget login. For example, suppose you make your car payment early and want to transfer the next deposit in that category to a different category. You could leave yourself a note to do just that, once you've done that and the note is no longer useful, simply delete it.
